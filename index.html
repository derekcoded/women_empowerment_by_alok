About

What is Women empowerment?

Women empowerment is about giving equal opportunities, respect, and freedom of choice to women in all aspects of life. Our mission is to celebrate inspiring stories, achievements, and provide resources that uplift and support women.

Through education, awareness, and community support, we're building a future where every woman can thrive and lead with confidence.

Women Who Changed the World

Kalpana Chawla

Field: Space Exploration
Born in Karnal, Haryana, Kalpana became the first Indian-born woman in space. She flew on Space Shuttle Columbia in 1997 and 2003, inspiring millions of Indian girls to dream beyond the sky. Her journey from a small town to NASA shows that dreams have no boundaries.

Mary Kom

Field: Sports
From a small village in Manipur, Mary became a six-time world boxing champion and Olympic medalist. She balanced motherhood with sports, proving women can excel in any field. She started boxing against her father's wishes and became India's pride.

Bachendri Pal

Field: Mountaineering
In 1984, Bachendri became the first Indian woman to reach Mount Everest's summit. Coming from a modest background, she led all-women expeditions and opened doors for women in adventure sports. She proved that courage knows no gender.

Gunjan Saxena

Field: Defense
Known as the "Kargil Girl," Gunjan flew combat missions during the 1999 Kargil War. She was one of India's first female combat pilots, breaking barriers in the armed forces. She rescued soldiers in dangerous war zones, proving women's strength in defense.

Tessy Thomas

Field: Science & Defense
Called the "Missile Woman of India," Tessy became the first woman to head an Indian missile project. She led the Agni-V missile team, showcasing women's excellence in STEM. She broke stereotypes in a male-dominated field.

Falguni Nayar

Field: Entrepreneurship
At 50, Falguni left her investment banking career to start Nykaa. She built India's largest beauty e-commerce platform, becoming one of the country's richest self-made women. She proved it's never too late to chase your dreams.

Arundhati Bhattacharya

Field: Banking & Finance
Arundhati became the first woman to chair the State Bank of India, managing the country's largest bank. She led digital transformation and broke the glass ceiling in Indian banking sector.

Kiran Mazumdar-Shaw

Field: Biotechnology
Started Biocon from her garage in 1978, Kiran built it into India's largest biopharmaceutical company. She became India's first self-made woman billionaire, revolutionizing affordable healthcare.

Inspiring Stories From Uttarakhand

Bachendri Pal

In 1984, Bachendri Pal became the first Indian woman to climb Mount Everest. Coming from a modest background in Uttarakhand, she broke barriers in the male-dominated field of mountaineering. Her achievement inspired generations of women to pursue adventure sports and chase impossible dreams. She remains a symbol of courage and endurance

Gaura Devi

Gaura Devi, leader of the Chipko Movement in the 1970s, stood up against deforestation in Uttarakhand. Along with village women, she hugged trees to protect them from being cut. Her bravery started a people’s movement for environmental protection and women’s role in saving nature. She is remembered as a true eco-warrior.

Tilu Rauteli

Tilu Rauteli, a brave warrior from Uttarakhand, is remembered for her courage at just 15 years old. After losing her father and brothers in battle, she took up arms against the Katyuri invaders. She fought seven consecutive battles, earning respect as a fearless symbol of woman power. Her story continues to inspire girls to rise above challenges and protect their dignity.

Madhuri Barthwal

Madhuri Barthwal is the first woman to be a music composer in All India Radio. She is said to be the first female musician from Uttarakhand to become a music teacher. On International Women's Day in 2019 she was awarded the Nari Shakti Puraskar by Ram Nath Kovind

Radha Behn (Radha Bhatt)

A Gandhian social worker from Kumaon, she dedicated her life to rural development, women empowerment, and environmental protection. She was also chairperson of Gandhi Peace Foundation.

Government's Role In Empowering Women

Beti Bachao, Beti Padhao

Launched in 2015, this initiative focuses on preventing gender-biased sex-selective elimination, ensuring survival, protection, and education of the girl child.

Mahila E-Haat

An online marketing platform launched by the Ministry of Women and Child Development to support women entrepreneurs and self-help groups in showcasing their products.

One Stop Centre Scheme

This scheme provides integrated support and assistance to women affected by violence, offering medical, legal, psychological, and counseling services.

Pradhan Mantri Matru Vandana Yojana

A maternity benefit program that provides financial aid to pregnant women and lactating mothers for better health and nutrition.

Pradhan Mantri Mudra Yojana

Aims to provide financial assistance to non-corporate, non-farm micro and small enterprises, with a significant portion of loans going to women entrepreneurs, according to the PIB.

Mahila Kisan Sashaktikaran Pariyojana

Focuses on empowering women in agriculture through training and capacity building, according to the Ministry of Rural Development.
